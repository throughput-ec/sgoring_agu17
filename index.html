Data Friction is a
"Time-To-Science" Problem

All recognize metadata’s potential value, but when the rubber meets the road, an unfunded mandate to be altruistic (and simultaneously to lose one’s own tried-and-true local bricolage with data structures) does not prove highly attractive. Introduced in order to reduce data friction, metadata creates its own kind of friction.
